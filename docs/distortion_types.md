# Types of Distortions

**A classification system for what happens to Hebrew concepts when translated into Greek, Latin, Slavonic, and Russian.**

---

## Overview

When a Hebrew word passes through foreign linguistic filters, its meaning rarely survives intact. The changes are not random — they follow predictable patterns.

This document classifies the **seven major types of distortion** that occur in biblical translation. Each type describes a specific way that concrete Hebrew meaning becomes abstracted, juridified, or inverted.

---

## The Seven Types of Distortion

| # | Type | Direction | Example |
|---|------|-----------|---------|
| 1 | Category Substitution | Relationship → Institution | Torah (guidance) → Law (code) |
| 2 | Juridification | Living bond → Legal contract | Brit (covenant bond) → Testament |
| 3 | Psychologization | Action → Inner feeling | Teshuvah (return) → Repentance (guilt) |
| 4 | Action → Emotion | Doing → Feeling | Chesed (loyal act) → Mercy (pity) |
| 5 | Abstraction | Concrete → Abstract | Emet (reliability) → Truth (philosophical) |
| 6 | Meaning Narrowing | Broad → Specific | Tzedakah (righteousness) → Alms (charity) |
| 7 | Dualization | Unified → Opposed | Torah (life) → Law (letter vs. spirit) |

---

## 1. Category Substitution (החלפת קטגוריה)

**What happens:** A relational concept becomes an institutional concept.

**Direction:** Relationship → Institution

**Hebrew character:** Personal, dynamic, relational

**Greek/Modern character:** Impersonal, static, structural

**Example:** תּוֹרָה (Torah)

| Hebrew | Greek/Russian |
|--------|----------------|
| Instruction from Father to child | Code of laws |
| Guidance for the path | External norm |
| Relationship of trust | Juridical obligation |

**Other candidates:** ברית (covenant bond → legal contract), קרבן (drawing near → sacrificial system)

---

## 2. Juridification (הפיכה לחוק)

**What happens:** A living bond becomes a legal contract. Something organic becomes codified.

**Direction:** Living bond → Legal contract

**Hebrew character:** Blood relationship, binding love, personal obligation

**Greek/Modern character:** Legal agreement, terms and conditions, enforceable by court

**Example:** בְּרִית (Brit)

| Hebrew | Greek/Russian |
|--------|----------------|
| Covenant bond (like marriage or blood brotherhood) | Testament, will, legal agreement |
| Founded on loyalty and love | Founded on terms and penalties |
| Breach leads to broken relationship, not lawsuit | Breach leads to legal penalty |

**Key problem:** Greek διαθήκη (testament, will) and Russian "завет" lose the sense of two becoming one.

---

## 3. Psychologization (הפיכה לפסיכולוגיה)

**What happens:** An external action becomes an internal feeling. Something you *do* becomes something you *feel*.

**Direction:** External action → Internal emotion

**Hebrew character:** Concrete, observable, physical action

**Greek/Modern character:** Subjective, emotional, internal state

**Example:** תְּשׁוּבָה (Teshuvah)

| Hebrew | Greek/Russian |
|--------|----------------|
| Returning — physically turning around and walking back | Repentance — feeling sorry, regretting |
| You can see someone return (they change direction) | You cannot see repentance (it is internal) |
| Action-oriented | Emotion-oriented |

**Key problem:** Repentance can be faked (I feel sorry but don't change). Return cannot be faked — you either turn around or you don't.

---

## 4. Action → Emotion (מעשה → רגש)

**What happens:** A loyal, obligatory action becomes a feeling of compassion or pity.

**Direction:** Doing → Feeling

**Hebrew character:** Act of will, obligation, loyalty shown through deeds

**Greek/Modern character:** Spontaneous emotion, compassion, "feeling sorry for"

**Example:** חֶסֶד (Chesed)

| Hebrew | Greek/Russian |
|--------|----------------|
| Covenant loyalty — doing what you promised because you are bound by love | Mercy — feeling pity for someone below you |
| An action (helping, protecting, delivering) | An emotion (compassion, sympathy) |
| Can be demanded (you are obligated) | Cannot be demanded (it must be felt) |

**Key problem:** When God's hesed becomes "mercy," His loyalty becomes optional emotion. But God's hesed is not optional — He is bound by His covenant.

---

## 5. Abstraction (הפיכה למופשט)

**What happens:** A concrete, physical concept becomes an abstract, philosophical one.

**Direction:** Concrete → Abstract

**Hebrew character:** Tangible, physical, grounded in senses

**Greek/Modern character:** Intellectual, philosophical, metaphysical

**Example:** אֱמֶת (Emet)

| Hebrew | Greek/Russian |
|--------|----------------|
| Reliability — something you can lean on, that is firm and trustworthy | Truth — correspondence between statement and reality |
| Concrete: a pillar is אמת, a faithful witness is אמת | Abstract: a proposition can be true |
| You can build a house on אמת | You cannot build a house on "truth" |

**Key problem:** When Pilate asks "What is truth?" (abstract philosophical question) — the Hebrew answer would be: "I am standing on it. It is reliable ground."

---

## 6. Meaning Narrowing (צמצום משמעות)

**What happens:** A broad, rich concept becomes a narrow, specific one.

**Direction:** Broad → Specific

**Hebrew character:** Wide semantic field, multiple related meanings

**Greek/Modern character:** Narrow, specialized meaning

**Example:** צְדָקָה (Tzedakah)

| Hebrew | Greek/Russian |
|--------|----------------|
| Righteousness, justice, restoring right order | Alms, charity, giving to the poor |
| Social justice, legal justice, personal righteousness, charitable giving | Only charitable giving |
| What God does (He is righteous) | What humans do (give money) |

**Key problem:** When tzedakah becomes "alms," the entire biblical concept of justice collapses into occasional charity.

---

## 7. Dualization (הפיכה לדואליזם)

**What happens:** A unified concept is split into opposing poles. Greek dualism (spirit vs. matter, grace vs. law, Old vs. New) replaces Hebrew wholeness.

**Direction:** Unified → Opposed

**Hebrew character:** Wholeness, both/and, integration

**Greek/Modern character:** Dualism, either/or, opposition

**Examples:**

| Hebrew Concept | Greek/Christian Opposition |
|----------------|---------------------------|
| Torah (instruction for life) | Law (Old) vs. Grace (New) |
| Spirit and flesh (both from God) | Spirit (good) vs. Flesh (evil) |
| This age and the age to come (continuity) | This world (evil) vs. Heaven (good) |
| Justice and mercy (both required) | Justice (harsh) vs. Mercy (kind) |

**Key problem:** Hebrew thought holds opposites together. Greek thought splits them into warring factions.

---

## Combining Types

A single concept may suffer multiple distortions:

| Concept | Primary Distortion | Secondary |
|---------|-------------------|-----------|
| Torah | Category Substitution (guidance → law code) | Dualization (law vs. grace) |
| Chesed | Action → Emotion (loyalty → pity) | Abstraction (covenant loyalty → abstract mercy) |
| Teshuvah | Psychologization (return → guilt) | Juridification (return to God → legal pardon) |
| Emet | Abstraction (reliability → truth) | Narrowing (faithful → propositionally true) |

---

## Why Classification Matters

**1. Diagnosis** — Before you can restore a concept, you must name what happened to it.

**2. Pattern recognition** — Once you know the types, you can spot them in new words without starting from zero.

**3. Translation critique** — You can evaluate a translation by asking: "Which types of distortion are present here?"

**4. Restoration strategy** — Each distortion type suggests its own remedy:

| Distortion | Remedy |
|------------|--------|
| Category Substitution | Return the relational frame |
| Juridification | Restore the living bond |
| Psychologization | Re-activate the action |
| Action → Emotion | Reconnect to deed, not feeling |
| Abstraction | Give concrete examples |
| Meaning Narrowing | Expand the semantic field |
| Dualization | Re-integrate the opposites |

---

## Examples for Practice

Try classifying these common distortions:

1. **"The law is a burden"** (applied to Torah)
   - Types involved: Category Substitution, Dualization

2. **"God, have mercy on me"** (when asking for loyalty-based help)
   - Types involved: Action → Emotion, maybe Narrowing

3. **"I repent of my sins"** (meaning "I feel guilty")
   - Types involved: Psychologization

4. **"That's not the truth"** (meaning "that statement is factually incorrect")
   - Types involved: Abstraction

5. **"Old Testament God of wrath vs. New Testament God of love"**
   - Types involved: Dualization (plus Category Substitution)
